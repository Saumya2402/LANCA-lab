Fundamental Research

Trailing Edge Geometry

We investigate the fundamental trade-offs between Sharp and Rounded trailing edges. While sharp edges maximize steady lift, rounded edges (common in manufacturing) introduce complex unsteady behaviors where the Kutta condition fails, delaying circulation buildup.

3D Flow Super-Resolution

We enhance low-resolution unsteady flows (e.g., Taylor–Green vortex on 128³ grids) using a 3D Attention-Augmented U-Net.

This model reconstructs fine-scale vorticity fields comparable to high-fidelity Remeshed Vortex Methods (RVM), enabling efficient modeling of turbulent coherent structures.

The Round Trailing Edge Family

Our study analyzes a family of Joukowski-derived airfoils with varying trailing edge bluntness.

Ratio 1 (Blunt)
Zero Initial Lift. Significant separation bubble.
Ratio 4 (Rounded)
Delayed Circulation. Stagnation point traverse required.
Ratio 100 (Sharp)
Wagner Effect. Instant 50% lift generation.
