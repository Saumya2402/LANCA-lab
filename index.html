Fundamental Research

Trailing Edge Geometry

We investigate the fundamental trade-offs between Sharp and Rounded trailing edges.

While sharp edges maximize steady lift, rounded edges (common in manufacturing) introduce complex unsteady behaviors where the Kutta condition fails, delaying circulation buildup.

Computational Research

GPU Accelerated Simulation

We enhance low-resolution unsteady flows (e.g., Taylor–Green vortex on 128³ grids) using a 3D Attention-Augmented U-Net.

This data-driven super-resolution model reconstructs fine-scale vorticity fields comparable to high-fidelity Remeshed Vortex Methods (RVM), enabling rapid modeling of turbulent structures.

The Round Trailing Edge Family

Our study analyzes a family of Joukowski-derived airfoils with varying trailing edge bluntness. The geometric "Ratio" determines the sharpness—Ratio 100 being nearly sharp, and Ratio 1 being extremely blunt.

Ratio 1:12 (Blunt)
Zero Initial Lift. Significant separation bubble and no Kutta condition establishment.
Ratio 4:12 (Rounded)
Delayed Circulation. Stagnation point must traverse the rounded edge before lift builds.
Ratio 100:12 (Sharp)
Wagner Effect. Instantaneous 50% lift generation due to sharp TE singularity.
